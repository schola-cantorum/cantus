# Code of Conduct

Cantus is a teaching project. Most people who read this repository are students,
instructors, or first-time contributors, so the bar we hold ourselves to is
simple: treat others the way you would want a patient teacher to treat you.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in the cantus community a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behaviour

- Assume good faith. A confused question is not an attack; a terse answer is not
  contempt.
- Give feedback on code and ideas, not on people. "This function mutates shared
  state" is useful; "you clearly don't understand Python" is not.
- Accept that contributors have different skill levels. Explaining a basic
  concept kindly is part of the work here.
- Respect the project's scope and the maintainers' time.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments, public or private.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sexualised language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or reviews.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — the repository, issues,
pull requests, and discussions — and when an individual represents the project
in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, report it privately to the
maintainers by opening a [private security advisory](https://github.com/schola-cantorum/cantus/security/advisories/new)
(it doubles as a confidential maintainer inbox) or by contacting a repository
maintainer directly through GitHub. Reports are handled confidentially. The
maintainers will review every report and respond as promptly as the project's
volunteer capacity allows.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and
other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and may temporarily or
permanently ban any contributor for behaviour they deem inappropriate.
